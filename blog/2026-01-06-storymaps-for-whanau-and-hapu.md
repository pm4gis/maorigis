StoryMaps work best when they are treated as narratives, not dashboards.

ArcGIS StoryMaps:
https://storymaps.arcgis.com/

For Māori audiences, less is often more. A small number of well chosen maps, photos, and short blocks of text usually land better than long technical explanations.

StoryMaps are useful for sharing progress, explaining context to funders, or recording journeys over time. They are not always appropriate for sensitive data.

Before publishing, always ask who the audience is and what access they should have. ArcGIS allows private and shared access options, which are often underused.

A StoryMap should invite reading, not demand it.
